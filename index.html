🍳 Kitchen Tips & Techniques

Time-tested advice from America's home kitchens — passed down for generations.

Cast Iron
🍳

Season Your Cast Iron the Right Way

Coat with a thin layer of flaxseed oil and bake upside-down at 500°F for 1 hour. Repeat 3–4 times for a non-stick surface that lasts decades.

Meat
🥩

Always Rest Your Meat

Let steak rest at least 5 minutes after cooking. This lets the juices redistribute — cutting too soon means dry, flavorless meat every time.

Baking
🧈

Room-Temperature Butter Matters

Cold butter won't cream properly with sugar. Pull it out 30–60 minutes before baking for cakes and cookies that bake up tall and fluffy.

Flavor
🧄

Bloom Your Spices First

Toast dry spices in a dry pan for 30 seconds before adding oil or liquid. Heat releases the essential oils and doubles the depth of flavor.

Pasta
🍝

Salt Your Pasta Water Generously

It should taste like the sea — about 1 tablespoon per quart. This is the only chance you have to season pasta from the inside out.

Knife Skills
🔪

A Sharp Knife Is a Safe Knife

Dull knives slip and require more force, causing accidents. Hone your knife before every use and sharpen it every 2–3 months.

Eggs
🥚

Low & Slow for Perfect Scrambled Eggs

Use the lowest heat setting and a rubber spatula. Pull the pan off the heat before they look fully done — residual heat finishes the job.

Soup
🍲

Deglaze for the Best Pan Sauces

After browning meat, add wine, broth, or even water and scrape up the brown bits. That fond is pure concentrated flavor you'd be throwing away.
