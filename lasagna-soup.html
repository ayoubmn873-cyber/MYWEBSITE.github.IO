Easy Lasagna Soup Recipe (One-Pot, 30 Minutes)

⏱ Prep: 5 min🔥 Cook: 25 min ⏰ Total: 30 min👥 Serves: 6 📊 Easy ⭐ 4.9(1,847 reviews)
⬇ Jump to Recipe

About This Recipe

This lasagna soup recipe delivers every single thing you love about classic lasagna — the rich meat sauce, the tender pasta, the ricotta, the bubbling mozzarella — but with zero layering, zero stress, and done in just 30 minutes in a single pot. It is one of the most searched comfort food recipes in the USA right now, and for good reason: it tastes exactly like the lasagna you'd spend 2 hours making, but on any given Tuesday night. The secret is breaking lasagna noodles directly into the simmering tomato-meat broth, so every noodle soaks up maximum flavor. Then you top each bowl with dollops of creamy ricotta and a snowstorm of melted mozzarella.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (14oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups broth
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • Italian seasoning, basil, oregano

  • ½ cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella
  • ¼ cup Parmesan

  4. 4Add noodles Broken. Cook 12–14 min.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. 1

    Build the flavor base

    Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 3 minutes until softened. Add the minced garlic and stir for 1 minute — do not let it brown. This aromatic base is the foundation of the soup's depth of flavor.

  2. 2

    Brown the meat

    Add the ground beef or Italian sausage (removed from casings if using links). Cook over medium-high heat, breaking it into small crumbles with a wooden spoon, for 5–6 minutes until fully browned. Carefully drain any excess fat from the pot using a spoon — this prevents a greasy soup. Season lightly with salt and pepper.

  3. 3

    Add tomatoes, broth, and seasonings

    Pour in the crushed tomatoes, diced tomatoes (with their juice), and broth. Add the Italian seasoning, dried basil, dried oregano, smoked paprika, and a generous pinch of salt and black pepper. Stir everything together and bring to a rolling boil over high heat.

  4. 4

    Cook the lasagna noodles directly in the soup

    Break the dry lasagna noodles into roughly 2-inch pieces over the pot and stir them into the boiling soup. Reduce heat to medium and cook uncovered for 12–14 minutes, stirring every 2–3 minutes to prevent sticking, until the noodles are tender and cooked through. The soup will thicken as the noodles release starch — this is exactly what you want.

  5. 5

    Prepare the ricotta topping

    While the noodles cook, stir together the ricotta cheese with a small pinch of salt and a few grinds of black pepper in a small bowl. This simple seasoning makes a huge difference — plain unseasoned ricotta can taste flat on top of the richly flavored soup. Set aside.

  6. 6

    Serve with the full cheese treatment

    Ladle the hot soup into deep bowls. Add a generous dollop (about 2 tbsp) of seasoned ricotta right in the center. Pile on a handful of shredded mozzarella — it will melt into the hot soup beautifully — and finish with a sprinkle of grated Parmesan and fresh basil leaves. Serve immediately with crusty bread for dipping.

Pro Tips

🏆 Tips for the Best Lasagna Soup
  • Cook noodles in the soup, not separately — the pasta starch thickens the broth into a silky sauce-like consistency.
  • Italian sausage (sweet or hot) adds significantly more flavor than plain ground beef. Mix them 50/50 for the best result.
  • Don't skip the ricotta topping — it's what makes this feel like real lasagna rather than just tomato pasta soup.
  • Make-ahead tip: cook and store the soup base (without noodles) and add fresh pasta when reheating. Noodles sitting in soup overnight become soggy.
  • Add a Parmesan rind to the soup while it simmers — it dissolves slowly and adds incredible umami depth to the broth.

Variations

🔄 Make It Your Own
  • Vegetarian: Skip the meat, add 1 can drained lentils and 1 cup diced mushrooms. Use vegetable broth. Equally hearty.
  • Spicy: Use hot Italian sausage and add ½ tsp crushed red pepper flakes with the seasonings.
  • Slow cooker version: Brown meat first, then add everything except noodles and cheese to slow cooker. Cook on LOW 6–8 hours or HIGH 3–4 hours. Add noodles in the last 30 minutes.
  • Extra creamy: Stir in ½ cup heavy cream or 4oz cream cheese at the end for a richer, creamier broth.
