Best Fudgy Brownie Recipe From Scratch

⏱ Prep: 15 min🔥 Cook: 22 min⏰ Total: 37 min 👥 Makes: 16📊 Easy ⭐ 5.0(632 reviews)

About This Recipe

The search for the best fudgy brownie recipe ends here. These aren't cakey, they aren't dry, and they're nothing like a box mix. They're dense, intensely chocolatey, with a tissue-thin crinkly top that shatters when you bite in, a gooey center that stays soft for days, and two kinds of chocolate for maximum depth. The technique of whisking the eggs vigorously into the warm chocolate creates that characteristic shiny crust — the hallmark of a truly great brownie. One bowl, 35 minutes, and you'll never buy brownies again.

Why You'll Love It

  • One bowl, no mixer required — easier than any box mix
  • That iconic crinkly top comes from the technique, not magic
  • Stay fudgy for up to 5 days at room temperature (if they last that long)
  • Can be made gluten-free by swapping in 1:1 GF flour

Ingredients

  • 115g unsalted butter
  • 200g dark chocolate (60–70%)
  • 200g granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs + 1 egg yolk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 80g all-purpose flour
  • 30g unsweetened cocoa powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 100g chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. 1Prep pan Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C). Line an 8x8 pan with parchment paper.
  2. 2Melt chocolate & butter Melt together over a bain-marie until smooth and glossy; cool slightly.
  3. 3Add sugar & eggs Whisk in sugar; add eggs and yolk one at a time, whisking vigorously. Add vanilla.
  4. 4Fold in dry ingredients Sift flour, cocoa, and salt into bowl; fold until just combined; fold in chocolate chips.
  5. 5Bake Bake 20–22 min until edges are set but center still jiggles slightly.
  6. 6Cool completely Cool at least 1 hour before cutting with a clean sharp knife.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. 1

    Prep the pan and preheat the oven

    Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C). Line an 8x8-inch square baking pan with parchment paper, leaving overhang on two sides — this creates handles that make lifting the brownies out effortless and prevents tearing.

  2. 2

    Melt butter and chocolate

    Combine the chopped chocolate and butter in a heatproof bowl. Set over a pot of barely simmering water (bain-marie) and stir frequently until completely melted and glossy. Remove from heat. The mixture should be warm but not hot — you should comfortably hold your hand over it.

  3. 3

    Add sugar and whisk vigorously

    Whisk in the sugar until fully combined. Then add the eggs and extra yolk, one at a time, whisking vigorously for about 1 minute after each addition. This step is where the crinkly top comes from — the vigorous whisking dissolves the sugar and incorporates air into the batter. Add vanilla and whisk again.

  4. 4

    Fold in the dry ingredients

    Sift the flour, cocoa powder, and salt directly into the bowl. Fold gently with a spatula until just combined and no streaks of flour remain. Do not overmix. Fold in the chocolate chips.

  5. 5

    Bake — and underbake slightly

    Pour the batter into the prepared pan and spread evenly. Bake for 20–22 minutes. The key indicator: the edges should be set and pulling away from the sides, while the center should still have a very slight jiggle when you shake the pan. A toothpick inserted in the center should come out with moist crumbs (not wet batter, not clean).

  6. 6

    Cool completely before cutting

    This is the hardest step. Let the brownies cool in the pan for at least 1 hour. Cutting them warm will result in messy, crumbling squares. Use a sharp knife wiped clean between each cut for perfect edges.

Pro Tips

🏆 Secrets to the Perfect Brownie
  • Use high-quality chocolate (60–70% cacao) — it's the most important ingredient and its flavor is the foundation of the whole recipe.
  • The extra egg yolk is the fudginess secret. Fat from the yolk creates a denser, moister texture than using whole eggs alone.
  • Always underbake slightly. Brownies continue to cook from residual heat after you remove them from the oven.
  • For the cleanest cuts, refrigerate the cooled brownies for 30 minutes before slicing and use a hot, dry knife.

Frequently Asked Questions

Cakey brownies are usually caused by too much flour, too many eggs, or overbaking. Follow the recipe measurements precisely, and pull them from the oven when the center still jiggles slightly. Also, avoid adding baking powder — this recipe relies solely on eggs for lift, which produces a denser, fudgier result.
The crinkly top forms when the sugar dissolves into the eggs and creates a thin meringue-like layer on top during baking. For maximum crinkle: whisk the eggs and sugar very vigorously for at least 2 minutes after adding each egg, and use granulated (not brown) sugar. The thin shiny crust is a sign of a properly made brownie.
Stored in an airtight container at room temperature, these brownies stay fudgy for 4–5 days. For longer storage, wrap individual squares tightly in plastic wrap and freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature for 30 minutes before serving.
