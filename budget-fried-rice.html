Budget-Friendly Chicken Fried Rice

⏱ Prep: 5 min🔥 Cook: 15 min ⏰ Total: 20 min👥 Serves: 4 📊 Easy ⭐ 4.9(689 reviews)
⬇ Jump to Recipe

About This Recipe

This budget-friendly chicken fried rice feeds 4 people for under $10 and tastes genuinely better than takeout. The secret is high heat, day-old rice, and not overcrowding the pan — three things most takeout places do that most home cooks don't. Golden egg ribbons, juicy chicken pieces, crispy rice grains, and a perfectly seasoned soy-sesame sauce come together in just 20 minutes. It's the easy weeknight dinner your family will request every single week — and you'll love that cleanup is one pan.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups cooked rice (day-old)
  • 300g chicken breast, small dice
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. 1Cook chicken High heat, 1 tbsp oil, cook diced chicken 4–5 min. Remove.
  2. 2Garlic + veg Add oil, garlic 30 sec, then frozen veg 2 min.
  3. 3Scramble eggs Push veg aside, add eggs, scramble until just set.
  4. 4Add rice Add cold rice, break clumps, fry 3 min until golden.
  5. 5Sauce & serve Add chicken back, soy, oyster sauce, sesame oil. Toss 1 min.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. 1

    Cook the chicken on high heat

    Heat 1 tbsp vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over the highest heat your stove produces. Season the diced chicken with salt and pepper, add to the smoking-hot pan, and cook without stirring for 2 minutes to get a golden crust. Stir and cook 2–3 more minutes until cooked through. Remove to a plate and set aside.

  2. 2

    Cook garlic and vegetables

    Add the remaining tablespoon of oil to the same wok. Add minced garlic and cook 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the frozen peas and carrots directly from frozen — they'll steam and thaw quickly. Stir-fry 2 minutes.

  3. 3

    Scramble the eggs

    Push the vegetables to one side of the wok. Crack all 3 eggs into the empty side. Scramble with a spatula until the eggs are just set but still slightly wet — they'll finish cooking when mixed with the rice. Don't let them overcook or they'll become rubbery.

  4. 4

    Fry the rice

    Add all the cold rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps with your spatula. Spread into an even layer and leave untouched for 1 minute so the bottom gets slightly crispy and toasted. Then stir everything together — rice, egg, and vegetables — and fry for another 2 minutes.

  5. 5

    Season and serve

    Return the chicken to the pan. Pour the soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil evenly over everything. Toss and stir-fry for 1 final minute until everything is coated and glistening. Taste and adjust salt. Serve immediately, topped with sliced green onions.

Pro Tips

🏆 The Secrets to Restaurant-Quality Fried Rice
  • Day-old rice is non-negotiable for the best texture. Fresh rice is too wet and steams rather than fries — resulting in mushy fried rice. Cook rice the day before and refrigerate uncovered overnight.
  • Use the highest heat possible. This is called "wok hei" — the charred, smoky flavor that makes takeout fried rice taste special. A screaming hot pan is the most important step.
  • Don't crowd the pan — cook in batches if your wok is small. Overcrowding drops the temperature and causes steaming instead of frying.
  • Add the sesame oil at the very end — it has a low smoke point and loses its flavor if cooked too long.

Delicious Variations

🔄 Ways to Switch It Up
  • Shrimp Fried Rice: Replace chicken with peeled shrimp — they cook in 2–3 minutes.
  • Vegetarian: Skip the chicken and add extra vegetables and tofu. Use a vegetarian oyster sauce.
  • Egg Fried Rice: The simplest version — just eggs, rice, soy sauce, and green onions. Incredibly delicious and costs under $2 to make.
  • Spicy: Add 1 tbsp Sriracha or sambal oelek with the sauces for a spicy kick.
