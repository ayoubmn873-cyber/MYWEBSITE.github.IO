Ingredients

  • 8 oz block feta (brine-packed)
  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • Toppings: honey, red pepper, herbs
  • Serve with: pita, veggies, crackers

  3. 3Blend 2–3 min until silky smooth.
  4. 4Taste & adjust Lemon, pepper, salt.
  5. 5Plate Swirl, drizzle oil, add toppings.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. 1

    Bring to room temperature

    Take the block feta out of its brine and pat it dry. Let both the feta and cream cheese sit at room temperature for 10–15 minutes. This is the most important tip for achieving a perfectly smooth result — cold cheese stays lumpy even after blending for several minutes. While you wait, squeeze your lemon juice and peel your garlic clove.

  2. 2

    Load the food processor

    Crumble the feta into the food processor bowl. Add the softened cream cheese, olive oil, fresh lemon juice, garlic clove, and black pepper. Make sure to use good quality extra-virgin olive oil here — it is a key flavor component, not just a texture helper. The garlic adds subtle depth without tasting raw or pungent after blending.

  3. 3

    Blend until perfectly silky

    Process continuously for 2–3 minutes, stopping once to scrape down the sides of the bowl with a rubber spatula. Initially the mixture will look crumbly and rough — keep going. After about 90 seconds it will start to come together. By 2–3 minutes it should be completely smooth, white, and fluffy with the consistency of thick whipped cream. Taste and adjust: more lemon for brightness, more pepper for depth.

  4. 4

    Plate and top it beautifully

    Scoop the whipped feta into a wide, shallow serving bowl. Use the back of a spoon to create elegant swirls and a small well in the center — this is where the olive oil and toppings will pool. Drizzle generously with your best olive oil. Choose your toppings: a drizzle of honey + crushed red pepper flakes is the most popular combination. Fresh thyme, Kalamata olives, roasted cherry tomatoes, or za'atar are all excellent alternatives.

  5. 5

    Serve immediately

    Serve the whipped feta right away with warm pita bread, pita chips, cucumber rounds, cherry tomatoes, and any crunchy vegetables you have on hand. Set extra olive oil on the table for drizzling. The dip can also be refrigerated immediately and served cold — it firms up slightly but is equally delicious and stores for up to 5 days.

Pro Tips

🏆 Tips for the Best Whipped Feta
  • Always use block feta packed in brine — pre-crumbled feta in a dry container is too dry and won't blend as smooth.
  • Greek feta (made from sheep's milk) tastes significantly better than regular cow's milk feta. Look for PDO (Protected Designation of Origin) on the label.
  • Room temperature ingredients are non-negotiable for silky-smooth results. Cold feta = lumpy dip.
  • Process for the full 2–3 minutes even if it looks smooth after 1 minute — the extra time develops a lighter, fluffier texture.
  • The honey + red pepper combination (sweet + spicy) is overwhelmingly the most popular topping variation. Don't skip it.

Variations

🔄 8 Ways to Top Your Whipped Feta
  • Classic: Olive oil + crushed red pepper flakes + fresh thyme
  • Sweet-spicy (most popular): Hot honey + red pepper flakes + sea salt
  • Mediterranean: Kalamata olives + cherry tomatoes + fresh oregano
  • Sun-dried tomato: Blend 3 tbsp sun-dried tomatoes into the feta for a rosy, sweet version
  • Herb garden: Fresh dill + cucumber + lemon zest + green onion
  • Roasted tomato: Top with slow-roasted cherry tomatoes and basil
  • Za'atar: Drizzle olive oil + generous sprinkle of za'atar spice blend
  • Spicy: Add harissa paste or roasted red peppers into the blend
