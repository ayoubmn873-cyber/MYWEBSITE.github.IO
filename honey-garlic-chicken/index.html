Crispy Honey Garlic Chicken (Better Than Takeout!)

⏱ Prep: 10 min🔥 Cook: 20 min⏰ Total: 30 min 👥 Serves: 4📊 Easy ⭐ 4.9(743 reviews)
⬇ Jump to Recipe

About This Recipe

Crispy honey garlic chicken is the weeknight dinner that everyone goes back for seconds on. Pan-seared chicken thighs develop a shatteringly crispy golden skin, then get finished in a glossy, sticky sauce made from honey, soy sauce, and a mountain of fresh garlic. The whole thing comes together in one pan in 30 minutes — no deep frying, no complicated technique, just big bold flavors that beat your local takeout every single time.

Why You'll Love It

  • One pan, 30 minutes — a complete weeknight dinner with zero fuss
  • The crispiest skin you've ever made at home, no deep fryer needed
  • Sticky honey garlic sauce that doubles as a glaze for the rice or veggies underneath
  • Budget-friendly — chicken thighs cost a fraction of breasts and taste better

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. 1

    Season the chicken

    Pat the chicken thighs completely dry with paper towels — this is the single most important step for crispy skin. Moisture is the enemy of crispiness. Season generously on both sides with salt, black pepper, garlic powder, and smoked paprika. Let sit at room temperature for 5 minutes.

  2. 2

    Sear skin-side down — do not touch

    Heat olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet (cast iron is ideal) over medium-high heat until shimmering. Place chicken thighs skin-side down. Do NOT move them for 8 full minutes. The skin needs uninterrupted contact with the hot pan to render the fat and turn golden and crispy. You'll see the skin shrink and pull away from the edges — that's perfect.

  3. 3

    Flip and sear the bottom

    Flip the thighs and sear for 3 more minutes on the other side. The chicken won't be cooked through yet — that's fine, it finishes in the oven. Transfer the chicken to a plate and set aside.

  4. 4

    Build the honey garlic sauce

    Keep the heat at medium. Add minced garlic to the same pan and sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant — watch it carefully, garlic burns fast. Pour in the honey, soy sauce, and apple cider vinegar. Stir everything together and bring to a simmer, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan. Those bits are pure flavor.

  5. 5

    Thicken the sauce

    Stir together the cornstarch and water in a small bowl to make a slurry. Pour it into the simmering sauce and stir continuously for about 1 minute until the sauce thickens to a glossy, sticky glaze consistency. It should coat the back of a spoon.

  6. 6

    Glaze and finish in the oven

    Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). Return the seared chicken to the pan, skin-side up. Spoon the honey garlic sauce generously over each piece. Transfer the pan to the oven and roast for 10 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 74°C (165°F). The sauce will caramelize and the skin will stay perfectly crispy.

  7. 7

    Serve and garnish

    Spoon any remaining sauce from the pan over the chicken. Garnish with sliced green onions and toasted sesame seeds. Serve over steamed white rice or alongside roasted vegetables to soak up all that incredible sauce.

Pro Tips

🏆 Tips for the Crispiest Chicken
  • Dry the chicken thoroughly before cooking — even a tiny bit of surface moisture creates steam instead of a sear.
  • Use bone-in thighs over boneless. The bone conducts heat evenly and the extra fat keeps the meat incredibly juicy.
  • Don't crowd the pan. If your skillet is small, work in two batches — crowding drops the temperature and causes steaming instead of searing.
  • Let the sauce cool slightly before serving — it thickens further as it cools and clings better to the chicken.
