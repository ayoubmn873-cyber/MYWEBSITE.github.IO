Baked Feta Pasta — The Original Viral TikTok Recipe

⏱ Prep: 5 min🔥 Cook: 35 min⏰ Total: 40 min👥 Serves: 4📊 Easy ⭐ 4.9(9,814 reviews)
⬇ Jump to Recipe

About This Recipe

Baked feta pasta is the recipe that broke the internet in 2021 — a Finnish dish that went so viral on TikTok that it reportedly caused feta cheese shortages across the USA and Europe. And it deserved every bit of the hype. A whole block of feta cheese and cherry tomatoes are roasted together in olive oil until the feta is golden and creamy and the tomatoes are burst and caramelized. You smash everything together, toss it with pasta and fresh basil, and you have one of the most effortlessly impressive pasta dishes ever invented. The sauce is creamy, briny from the feta, sweet from the roasted tomatoes, and utterly addictive. The whole recipe takes 5 minutes of hands-on time.

Ingredients

  • 1 block (7–8oz) feta cheese
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes
  • 3–4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp red pepper flakes
  • 10oz pasta of choice
  • Fresh basil
  • Salt and black pepper

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. 2Add tomatoes + garlic to baking dish with oil.
  3. 3Place feta block in center. Drizzle with oil.
  4. 4Bake 30–35 min until golden and blistered.
  5. 5Cook pasta al dente. Reserve ½ cup water.
  6. 6Smash feta + tomatoes. Toss with pasta + basil.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. 1

    Set up the baking dish

    Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Pour the cherry tomatoes into a 9×13 inch baking dish. Add the smashed garlic cloves. Drizzle 3 tbsp of olive oil over everything. Season generously with red pepper flakes and black pepper. Toss to coat all the tomatoes in the oil.

  2. 2

    Add the feta block to the center

    Place the full block of feta cheese right in the center of the tomatoes — do not crumble it. Drizzle 1 tbsp olive oil over the top of the feta block. Add a grind of black pepper on top. The feta should be surrounded by tomatoes on all sides. Do not add salt — feta is already very salty and will season the entire dish as it bakes.

  3. 3

    Bake until everything is golden and caramelized

    Bake at 400°F for 30–35 minutes. The tomatoes should be completely blistered, burst open, and starting to caramelize at the edges. The feta should be golden on top and completely soft in the center — it should jiggle slightly when you shake the dish, like a soft cheesecake. This is exactly what you want. Do not underbake — raw-ish feta will not mash into a creamy sauce.

  4. 4

    Cook the pasta while the feta bakes

    Bring a large pot of water to a boil and salt it heavily (it should taste like the sea). Cook your pasta until al dente — slightly firmer than you want to eat it, since it will soften more when tossed with the hot sauce. Before draining, scoop out ½ cup of the cloudy, starchy pasta water. This pasta water is key to making the sauce glossy and coating the pasta perfectly.

  5. 5

    Smash, toss, and serve

    Remove the baking dish from the oven. Using a fork, smash the feta block and burst tomatoes together, stirring vigorously until they form a chunky, creamy sauce. Add a splash of reserved pasta water and stir to loosen the sauce. Add the drained pasta to the dish and toss well until every piece is coated in the creamy feta-tomato sauce. Add a big handful of torn fresh basil, taste for salt, and serve immediately.

Frequently Asked Questions

A block of feta packed in brine bakes into a creamy, smashable consistency. Crumbled feta contains anti-caking agents and dries out instead of melting. Always use a whole block — Bulgarian or Greek feta works best. Trader Joe's and Whole Foods carry it year-round.
Short tube shapes that trap the sauce inside are best — penne, rigatoni, cavatappi, or fusilli. Long pasta works but does not hold the chunky feta sauce as effectively. Cook al dente since the pasta continues to soften in the hot sauce.
Yes — grilled chicken, shrimp, or Italian sausage are the most popular additions. Add them directly to the baking dish with the tomatoes and feta, or cook separately and toss in at the end. Chickpeas roasted in the dish alongside the tomatoes make a great vegetarian protein addition.
