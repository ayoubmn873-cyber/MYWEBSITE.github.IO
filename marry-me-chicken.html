Marry Me Chicken Recipe (One-Pan, 30 Minutes)

⏱ Prep: 5 min🔥 Cook: 25 min⏰ Total: 30 min👥 Serves: 4📊 Easy ⭐ 4.9(8,743 reviews)

Marry Me Chicken is the one-pan dinner that reportedly leads to marriage proposals. Legend has it the dish got its name after a food editor tasted it and said "I'd marry whoever made this." That story went viral — and it went viral because the recipe genuinely delivers. Tender seared chicken simmered in a rich, creamy sun-dried tomato parmesan sauce with garlic and herbs. Ingredients

  • 4 chicken breasts
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ½ cup chicken broth
  • ⅓ cup sun-dried tomatoes
  • ½ cup Parmesan, grated
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Thyme, oregano, chili flakes
  • Salt, pepper, fresh basil

Instructions

  1. 1Season chicken with salt, thyme, oregano.
  2. 2Sear 4–5 min per side until golden.
  3. 3Sauté garlic + sun-dried tomatoes.
  4. 4Add broth + cream + chili flakes.
  5. 5Melt in Parmesan until creamy.
  6. 6Return chicken, simmer 8–10 min. Serve.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. 1

    Season and prep the chicken

    Pat the chicken breasts completely dry — this is critical for a good golden sear. Season generously on both sides with salt, black pepper, dried thyme, and dried oregano. If your chicken breasts are very thick (over 1 inch), butterfly them or pound them to even thickness so they cook evenly.

  2. 2

    Sear to a deep golden crust

    Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat until the oil shimmers. Add the chicken and cook without moving for 4–5 minutes until a deep golden crust forms. Flip and sear the other side 4–5 minutes. Transfer to a plate — the chicken will not be fully cooked through yet. This partial cooking and then finishing in the sauce is what keeps it incredibly juicy.

  3. 3

    Build the sauce in the same pan

    Reduce heat to medium. Add minced garlic to the pan drippings and stir for 60 seconds until fragrant. Add the roughly chopped sun-dried tomatoes and stir 30 seconds. Pour in the chicken broth and scrape up all the golden brown bits stuck to the bottom of the pan — this is pure flavor. Add heavy cream and red pepper flakes.

  4. 4

    Create the creamy parmesan sauce

    Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer. Add grated Parmesan in two additions, stirring between each until fully melted and the sauce becomes smooth and glossy. Taste and adjust salt and pepper. The sauce should coat the back of a spoon. If it is too thick, add a splash more broth. If too thin, simmer 2–3 minutes more.

  5. 5

    Finish the chicken in the sauce

    Return the seared chicken to the skillet and spoon the sauce generously over the top. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 8–10 minutes until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C). The chicken finishes cooking while absorbing all the flavors from the sauce. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and extra Parmesan. Serve immediately.

Pro Tips

🏆 Tips for Perfect Marry Me Chicken
  • Dry the chicken completely before searing — moisture = steam = no golden crust.
  • Do not rush the sear. A deep golden crust adds enormous flavor to the final dish.
  • Use sun-dried tomatoes packed in oil — they are far more flavorful than dry-packed.
  • Freshly grated Parmesan melts much more smoothly than pre-shredded (which has anti-caking agents).
  • Serve with pasta: toss 8oz of cooked linguine directly in the sauce before adding the chicken back.

Frequently Asked Questions

The name reportedly comes from a food editor who tasted the dish and said "I'd marry whoever made this." The story went viral on TikTok and Instagram — and the recipe genuinely earns the hype. The combination of creamy sauce, golden seared chicken, and sun-dried tomatoes is one of those dishes that makes people stop mid-bite.
Boneless skinless chicken thighs are actually juicier and more flavorful than breasts for this recipe. Cook them 5–6 min per side to sear, then simmer 12–14 min in the sauce until 165°F. The thigh fat adds incredible richness to the already creamy sauce.
The sauce is outstanding over pasta (linguine or penne), mashed potatoes, or rice. Crusty bread for dipping is essential. For a low-carb option, serve over zucchini noodles or cauliflower rice. Roasted asparagus or a simple green salad on the side completes the meal perfectly.
